Biomedical Ontology

Biomedical Knowledge System

🧬 Genes

Genes contain biological information that influences how organisms develop and how certain diseases occur.

🦠 Diseases

Diseases represent medical conditions that affect normal body functions and require diagnosis and treatment.

🤒 Symptoms

Symptoms are observable signs that help identify the presence of diseases in a patient.

💊 Treatment

Treatments include medicines, therapies, and procedures used to manage or cure diseases.

🧑‍⚕️ Patient

The patient is the individual receiving medical care and whose health data connects different biomedical concepts.

🧪 Research

Biomedical research studies diseases, genes, and treatments to improve healthcare and medical technologies.

Biomedical ontology organizes medical knowledge by defining how biological and medical concepts are related. Concepts such as genes, diseases, symptoms, treatments, and patients are connected within a structured system.

This organization allows medical databases, researchers, and intelligent healthcare systems to interpret biological information accurately and support better diagnosis, research, and treatment planning.
